Architecture Case Study 02 · Subscription Flow

🔄 Subscription Discount Lifecycle

How subscription-programs-app gates membership activation on incentive eligibility, KillBill plan enrollment, and discount calculation — and why an incentive-service outage blocks subscription revenue.

subscription-programs-appincentive-serviceKillBilldynamic_pricingorders-service
📖
Overview
subscription-programs-app manages the full membership lifecycle — from plan selection through KillBill billing to incentive activation. The critical insight from graph analysis: the service's largest community (78 nodes, community #0) is entirely dedicated to calling incentive-service, making it an implicit Tier-1 dependency.

💣
incentive-service outage directly blocks subscription membership activation
Steps 2 and 5 both call incentive-service synchronously. An incentive-service degradation blocks new membership enrollments — converting a pricing service issue into a subscription revenue impact. No circuit breaker exists on either call.
🔗
Discount Calculation Logic
3-service chain
🧮
Discount = IncentiveEvaluationOperation + KillBill plan tier + ML min-price floor
The final subscription discount is the intersection of: (1) incentive-service constraint evaluation (campaign eligibility, user cap, force-incentive flag), (2) KillBill plan discount tier, and (3) dynamic_pricing min-price floor from ML Toolkit. All three must agree.
🔄
Membership god-object (degree 197) bridges 30+ communities — highest structural risk
Membership class in subscription-programs-app has betweenness centrality 0.137 and connects KillBill, Business Metrics, Subscription Plans, and 30+ other communities. Any schema change propagates breakage platform-wide with zero isolation.
🔁
Rollback pipeline on payment failure re-enters incentive-service
Payment failure triggers CancelIncentivesStep which calls incentive-service /incentive/unuse. Under incentive-service degradation, both the forward path and the rollback path are blocked — leaving subscriptions in an inconsistent state.
✦
AI-Derived Insights
Graph-grounded
🔴
subscription-programs-app community #0 (78 nodes) is entirely incentive-service calls
The strongest cross-service coupling signal in the platform graph. This community contains ActivateIncentivesStep, CancelIncentivesStep, KbSubscriptionCreateStep, and all their dependencies — all routing through incentive-service.
⚠️
No circuit breaker on either incentive-service call in the activation pipeline
Both eligibility check (step 2) and incentive activation (step 5) call incentive-service synchronously without a Resilience4j circuit breaker. Recommend adding a 30ms timeout + cached fallback to both.
🎯
Key Takeaway
subscription discounts are calculated across 3 services with no circuit breaker protection. The Membership god-object is the highest structural risk — degree 197 with 0.137 betweenness means any Membership change has platform-wide blast radius. Priority: (1) add circuit breaker to ActivateIncentivesStep, (2) decompose Membership into SubscriptionStatus + BillingRecord + IncentiveActivationState.
